Research Interest

My research interest lies at the intersection of machine learning, computer vision, uncertainty, image processing, and optimization methods, with a focus on their application to medical information processing, healthcare, and the detection of neurological disorders such as Parkinson's Disease, Alzheimer's, and Acute Kidney Injury (AKI). I am particularly interested in enhancing the robustness of deep learning models against adversarial or noisy inputs to ensure accurate and timely medical diagnoses. This involves exploring the impact of model complexity, training data quality, and hyperparameters on model performance, as well as addressing security and privacy concerns through adversarial training, input preprocessing, data augmentation, and uncertainty estimation. Additionally, my work evaluates tools and frameworks that improve the resilience of AI systems in the medical domain, aiming for more trustworthy, reliable, and stable solutions. For a deeper understanding of my contributions to this field, please refer to the Publications section!

I am open to research discussion and collaboration, please feel free to get in touch!
